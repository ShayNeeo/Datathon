\subsection{1. Loyalty Collapse — Direction 02 (Customer Lifecycle)}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.72\textwidth]{figures/6_loyalty_collapse.png}
\caption{Next-year retention across annual customer cohorts, 2012--2021 (Direction 02).}
\label{fig:loyalty_collapse}
\vspace{-0.2cm}
\end{figure}

Our Direction 02 Customer Lifecycle analysis computed next-year retention for every acquisition cohort from 2012 to 2021 by joining \texttt{orders}, \texttt{customers}, and \texttt{order\_items} across order-date windows.
\textbf{Descriptive:} The 2012 cohort retained 64.7\% of customers the following year; the 2021 cohort retained only 6.7\%.
\textbf{Diagnostic:} The business has shifted from a community-driven brand to a transactional storefront. The acceleration after 2018 — confirmed in Figure~\ref{fig:appendix_customer_quality} (Appendix~B) — aligns with the Double-Day discount era, which attracted low-LTV bargain hunters.
\textbf{Predictive:} If the trend holds, Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) will exceed LTV within two years.
\textbf{Prescriptive:} Freeze broad acquisition spend; redirect to lifecycle CRM, post-purchase reactivation flows, and tiered loyalty rewards.

\subsection{2. Loss-Leader Trap — Direction 01 (Product Dominance)}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.72\textwidth]{figures/1_loss_leader_trap.png}
\caption{Basket economics: loss-leader orders vs.\ standard orders (Direction 01).}
\label{fig:loss_leader}
\vspace{-0.2cm}
\end{figure}

Direction 01 audited every SKU's unit margin using \texttt{products.cogs} and \texttt{products.price}, then linked to \texttt{order\_items} to measure downstream basket effects.
\textbf{Descriptive:} 461 SKUs operate at $\le$5.5\% gross margin; orders containing them average ${\sim}$24.2K VND revenue — but basket profit collapses to -1{,}473 VND versus +3{,}980 VND for standard orders.
\textbf{Diagnostic:} Cross-sell is failing: customers add only +0.22 extra items per basket. The strategy loses money without generating the compensating upsell it was designed to trigger. Figure~\ref{fig:appendix_margin_size} (Appendix~A) further shows that L/XL sizes carry the highest margin per unit — the assortment mix is skewed against the company's own best pockets.
\textbf{Predictive:} Continued loss-leader reliance will compress gross margin irreparably.
\textbf{Prescriptive:} Discontinue standalone loss-leader selling; gate purchases behind a basket-margin floor or hard bundle.

\subsection{3. Inelastic Logistics Paradox — Direction 03 (Operational Friction)}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.72\textwidth]{figures/2_logistics_paradox.png}
\caption{Repurchase rate vs.\ first-order delivery speed, 2012--2022 (Direction 03).}
\label{fig:logistics_paradox}
\vspace{-0.2cm}
\end{figure}

Direction 03 linked \texttt{shipments}, \texttt{orders}, and repeat-purchase flags derived from \texttt{order\_items} to test whether delivery speed predicts customer return.
\textbf{Descriptive:} The repurchase rate holds flat at ${\sim}$74\% across the full 2--14 day delivery spectrum.
\textbf{Diagnostic:} Brand loyalty, not logistics, drives repurchase; premium 3PL costs are wasted. The same direction's seasonal audit — see Figure~\ref{fig:appendix_tet} (Appendix~C) — shows Tet demand surges 40--80\% at T-30, then collapses, creating avoidable stockout stress that \emph{does} affect fulfilment quality.
\textbf{Predictive:} Maintaining express shipping SLAs will continue to drain logistics margin with zero LTV uplift.
\textbf{Prescriptive:} Switch non-urgent orders to economy tiers immediately. Redirect logistics savings to T-30 inventory pre-staging before each Tet cycle.

\subsection{4. Campaign ROI Paradox — Direction 04 (Financial Dynamics)}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.72\textwidth]{figures/5_campaign_roi.png}
\caption{Campaign lift vs.\ gross margin: Spring Sale succeeds, Year-End Sale destroys value (Direction 04).}
\label{fig:campaign_roi}
\vspace{-0.2cm}
\end{figure}

Direction 04 joined \texttt{promotions}, \texttt{order\_items}, and the \texttt{sales.csv} daily revenue series to isolate promotion-window effects.
\textbf{Descriptive:} Spring Sale lifts revenue +44.2\% while protecting 10.5\% gross margin. Year-End Sale cuts revenue -46.3\% and compresses margin to 1.5\%.
\textbf{Diagnostic:} Deep year-end discounting trains customers to wait for markdowns, suppressing baseline revenue during the window. Figure~\ref{fig:appendix_revenue_margin} (Appendix~D) confirms that revenue has grown 10$\times$ over the decade while gross margin has compressed — the discount habit is eroding the long-run financial baseline.
\textbf{Predictive:} Repeating Year-End mechanics will continue to destroy pricing power.
\textbf{Prescriptive:} Eliminate flat year-end markdowns; replace with tiered loyalty discounts and a hard 8\% gross-margin floor on all promotion events.
