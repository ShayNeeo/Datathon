\subsection{1. The Loyalty Collapse}

\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.72\textwidth]{../figures_living/part2_exceptional/6_loyalty_collapse.png}
\caption{Next-year retention across annual customer cohorts (2012--2021).}
\label{fig:loyalty_collapse}
\vspace{-0.2cm}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Descriptive:} Acquisition volume is climbing, but next-year retention has plummeted from 64.7\% (2012 cohort) to 6.7\% (2021 cohort). \textbf{Diagnostic:} The business has transitioned from a sticky, community-driven brand to a highly transactional storefront. \textbf{Predictive:} If this trajectory holds, Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) will rapidly exceed Customer Lifetime Value (LTV), suffocating cash flow. \textbf{Prescriptive:} Immediately freeze generic acquisition spend and redirect it toward lifecycle CRM, reactivation campaigns, and tiered loyalty rewards.

\subsection{2. The Loss-Leader Trap}

\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.72\textwidth]{../figures_living/part2_exceptional/1_loss_leader_trap.png}
\caption{Basket economics: Standard orders vs. orders containing loss-leader SKUs.}
\label{fig:loss_leader}
\vspace{-0.2cm}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Descriptive:} Orders containing extreme low-margin products ($\le$ 5.5\%) generate similar revenue ($\sim$24.2K VND) to standard orders, but their basket profit drops to \textit{-1,473 VND}. \textbf{Diagnostic:} The loss-leader strategy is failing. Customers buy the cheap item but only add +0.22 extra items to their cart, failing to cross-subsidize the loss. \textbf{Predictive:} Continued reliance on these 461 SKUs for volume will compress total gross margin irreparably. \textbf{Prescriptive:} Discontinue standalone sales of loss-leaders; they must be gated behind high \texttt{min\_order\_value} thresholds or hard-bundled.

\subsection{3. The Inelastic Logistics Paradox}

\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.72\textwidth]{../figures_living/part2_exceptional/2_logistics_paradox.png}
\caption{Customer repurchase rate plotted against first-order delivery time.}
\label{fig:logistics_paradox}
\vspace{-0.2cm}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Descriptive:} Repurchase rates remain completely flat at $\sim$74\% whether a customer receives their first order in 2 days or 14 days. \textbf{Diagnostic:} VinUni customers exhibit massive inelasticity regarding shipping speed; the brand strength overrides delivery delays. \textbf{Predictive:} Paying premium 3PL fees for expedited shipping will not yield higher LTV. \textbf{Prescriptive:} Downgrade non-urgent orders to economy shipping tiers to instantly recapture logistics margin without sacrificing customer loyalty.

\subsection{4. The Tet Demand Shock}

\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.72\textwidth]{../figures_living/part2_exceptional/4_tet_effect.png}
\caption{Revenue velocity indexed around the Lunar New Year (Day 0).}
\label{fig:tet_effect}
\vspace{-0.2cm}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Descriptive:} Demand surges 40--80\% starting 30 days before Tet, followed by a severe contraction during the holiday. \textbf{Diagnostic:} This is a structural calendar regime, causing massive stockout risks before the holiday and dead inventory during it. \textbf{Predictive:} Failing to front-load supply guarantees missed revenue and fulfillment bottlenecks. \textbf{Prescriptive:} Transition to a T-30 supply staging policy. Cut marketing spend at T-5 as logistics networks freeze.
