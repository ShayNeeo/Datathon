\subsection{1. Loyalty Collapse — Direction 02 (Customer Lifecycle)}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.72\textwidth]{figures/6_loyalty_collapse.png}
\caption{Next-year retention across annual customer cohorts, 2012--2021 (Direction 02).}
\label{fig:loyalty_collapse}
\vspace{-0.2cm}
\end{figure}

Our Direction 02 Customer Lifecycle analysis computed next-year retention for every acquisition cohort from 2012 to 2021 by joining \texttt{orders}, \texttt{customers}, and \texttt{order\_items} across order-date windows.
\textbf{Descriptive:} The 2012 cohort retained 64.7\% of customers the following year; the 2021 cohort retained only 6.7\%. Figure~\ref{fig:appendix_order_freq} (Appendix~B) confirms a massive single-order customer base.
\textbf{Diagnostic:} The business has shifted from a community-driven brand to a transactional storefront. The acceleration after 2018 — confirmed in Figure~\ref{fig:appendix_customer_quality} (Appendix~B) — aligns with the Double-Day discount era, which attracted low-LTV bargain hunters.
\textbf{Predictive:} If the trend holds, Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) will exceed LTV within two years.
\textbf{Prescriptive:} Freeze broad acquisition spend; redirect to lifecycle CRM, post-purchase reactivation flows, and tiered loyalty rewards.

\subsection{2. Star vs. Bait and the Loss-Leader Trap — Direction 01 (Product Dominance)}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.72\textwidth]{figures/star_vs_bait_analysis.png}
\caption{Star vs. Bait Portfolio: Margin vs. Volume by product line code (Direction 01).}
\label{fig:star_vs_bait}
\vspace{-0.2cm}
\end{figure}

Direction 01 evaluated SKU-level economics, mapping brand prefixes into a "Star vs. Bait" portfolio matrix based on \texttt{products.cogs} and \texttt{price}.
\textbf{Descriptive:} The UR line (Star) achieves 31.3\% margins, while YY/UC lines (Bait) linger around 23--24\%. At the SKU level (Figure~\ref{fig:loss_leader_trap}, Appendix~A), 461 items operate at $\le$5.5\% margin, destroying basket profit (-1{,}473 VND per order).
\textbf{Diagnostic:} The "Bait" lines and extreme loss-leaders fail to generate compensating cross-sell (+0.22 extra items per basket). The strategy loses money without triggering sufficient upsell.
\textbf{Predictive:} Continued reliance on Bait lines for volume will irreversibly compress total gross margin.
\textbf{Prescriptive:} Expand investment in the high-margin UR Star line. Discontinue standalone sales of deep loss-leaders by gating them behind a minimum basket-margin floor or hard bundle.

\subsection{3. Inelastic Logistics Paradox — Direction 03 (Operational Friction)}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.72\textwidth]{figures/2_logistics_paradox.png}
\caption{Repurchase rate vs.\ first-order delivery speed, 2012--2022 (Direction 03).}
\label{fig:logistics_paradox}
\vspace{-0.2cm}
\end{figure}

Direction 03 linked \texttt{shipments}, \texttt{orders}, and repeat-purchase flags derived from \texttt{order\_items} to test whether delivery speed predicts customer return.
\textbf{Descriptive:} The repurchase rate holds flat at ${\sim}$74\% across the full 2--14 day delivery spectrum.
\textbf{Diagnostic:} Brand loyalty, not logistics, drives repurchase; premium 3PL costs are wasted. The same direction's seasonal audit — see Figure~\ref{fig:appendix_tet} (Appendix~C) — shows Tet demand surges 40--80\% at T-30, then collapses, creating avoidable stockout stress that \emph{does} affect fulfilment quality.
\textbf{Predictive:} Maintaining express shipping SLAs will continue to drain logistics margin with zero LTV uplift.
\textbf{Prescriptive:} Switch non-urgent orders to economy tiers immediately. Redirect logistics savings to T-30 inventory pre-staging before each Tet cycle.

\subsection{4. Payment Engineering \& Campaign Paradox — Direction 04 (Financial Dynamics)}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.48\textwidth]{figures/installment_aov_boxplot.png}
\hfill
\includegraphics[width=0.48\textwidth]{figures/ltv_by_payment_method.png}
\caption{Left: AOV lift from Installment plans. Right: LTV by Payment Method (Direction 04).}
\label{fig:financial_engineering}
\vspace{-0.2cm}
\end{figure}

Direction 04 investigated financial flows by joining \texttt{payments}, \texttt{orders}, and \texttt{promotions}.
\textbf{Descriptive:} Installment plans lift Average Order Value (AOV) by +35\%. Furthermore, credit card users generate 2--3$\times$ higher Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) than Cash-on-Delivery (COD) users. Conversely, deep promotional discounting during the Year-End Sale (Figure~\ref{fig:appendix_campaign}, Appendix~D) drops revenue -46.3\% and compresses margin to 1.5\%.
\textbf{Diagnostic:} Payment methods directly select for customer quality and basket size, while poorly structured campaigns (flat markdowns) erode pricing power. Stackable promos (Figure~\ref{fig:appendix_stackability}, Appendix~D) frequently breach the 30\% margin dilution threshold.
\textbf{Predictive:} Over-reliance on COD and flat discounting will suffocate long-term financial baseline health.
\textbf{Prescriptive:} Feature installments as the default checkout option. Incentivize payment method upgrades from COD to credit cards. Replace flat markdowns with tiered rewards and a hard 8\% gross-margin floor.
